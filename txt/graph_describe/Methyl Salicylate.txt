Methyl salicylate is a naturally occurring organic compound that belongs to the class of salicylates. It is commonly known as wintergreen oil or oil of wintergreen, and has a characteristic sweet and minty aroma. Methyl salicylate is commonly used as a flavoring agent in the food and beverage industry, as well as in the production of perfumes, cosmetics, and topical analgesics.

In addition to its aromatic properties, methyl salicylate also has analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, and is often used as an active ingredient in pain relief ointments and topical creams. However, excessive use of methyl salicylate can lead to toxicity and overdose, and it should be used with caution under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
