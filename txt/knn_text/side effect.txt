Aspirin, Methyl Salicylate, and Salicylates are all salicylate drugs with similar pharmacological effects and side effects.

Here are some possible side effects of these drugs:

1. Gastrointestinal reactions: including stomach pain, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

2. Inhibition of coagulation function: these drugs can inhibit platelet aggregation and coagulation, increase the risk of bleeding and hematoma.

3. Allergic reactions: some people may have allergic reactions to these drugs, including skin itching, urticaria, difficulty breathing, and throat swelling.

4. Poisoning: Overuse of these drugs may cause poisoning, with symptoms such as headache, dizziness, tinnitus, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and metabolic acidosis.

5. Others: These drugs may also cause other side effects, such as insomnia, anxiety, depression, dry mouth, dizziness, edema, and hypertension.

It should be noted that the side effects of these drugs may vary due to individual differences, and some people may be more prone to side effects. If you experience any discomfort or symptoms while using these drugs, it is recommended to stop using them immediately and consult a doctor.
